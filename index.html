SpeechRefiner: Towards General Perceptual Quality Refinement for Front-End Algorithms

we can add the author here

Abstract: Front-end speech processing algorithms, such as denoising, de-reverberation, and speech separation, are widely adopted both as standalone applications and as preprocessing steps for downstream tasks. However, the processing can be insufficient, leaving residual noises or introducing new artifacts. These issues are often not reflected in terms of metrics such as SI-SNR but are more apparent in the perceptual quality experienced by listeners. In this work, we propose SpeechRefiner to tackle this problem. SpeechRefiner is a post-processor that can be integrated with different front-end algorithms. Our experiments demonstrate that the proposed model, trained on a single impairment source, can generalize to different ones, significantly improving the perceptual quality of the audio.
